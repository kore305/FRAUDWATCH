Recommended Books on Fraud Prevention

"The Art of Deception" by Kevin Mitnick

Learn about social engineering and the tactics used by hackers.

"Social Engineering: The Science of Human Hacking" by Christopher Hadnagy

A deep dive into the methods of social engineers and how to protect yourself.

"Spam Nation" by Brian Krebs

An investigation into the world of spam and cybercrime.

"Kingpin: How One Hacker Took Over the Billion-Dollar Cybercrime Underground" by Kevin Poulsen

A thrilling true story of a hacker's rise and fall in the cybercrime world.

"The Confidence Game: Why We Fall for It... Every Time" by Maria Konnikova

An exploration into the psychology behind why we fall for scams.

"Future Crimes" by Marc Goodman

A comprehensive look at how technology is being used for crime.

"The Cyber Effect" by Dr. Mary Aiken

An analysis of how our online lives impact our real-world behavior.

"Cybersecurity and Cyberwar" by P.W. Singer and Allan Friedman

A guide to understanding the basics of cybersecurity and cyber warfare.

"The Art of Intrusion" by Kevin Mitnick

Mitnick's account of real-life stories of hackers and the lessons we can learn from them.

"Hacking: The Art of Exploitation" by Jon Erickson

A technical guide to hacking and cybersecurity.

"Ghost in the Wires" by Kevin Mitnick

Mitnick's autobiography, detailing his experiences as a fugitive hacker.

"Cult of the Dead Cow" by Joseph Menn

The story of the notorious hacking group and their impact on cybersecurity.

"Phishing Dark Waters" by Christopher Hadnagy and Michele Fincher

A book on the dangers of phishing and how to protect against it.

"Click Here to Kill Everybody" by Bruce Schneier

A look at the risks of the connected world and what we can do to secure it.

"The Cuckoo's Egg" by Clifford Stoll

The story of how a lone astronomer tracked down a hacker.

"Zero Day" by Mark Russinovich

A fictional thriller based on real-world cybersecurity threats.

"This Is How They Tell Me the World Ends" by Nicole Perlroth

An investigation into the global cyber arms race.

"Blackhatonomics" by Will Gragido, Daniel Molina, John Pirc, and Nick Selby

An economic analysis of cybercrime and its impact.

"Malware, Rootkits & Botnets" by Christopher C. Elisan

An in-depth guide to understanding and defending against malware.
